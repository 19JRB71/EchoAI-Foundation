Here's Prompt 7: Lead Management and AI Qualification Chatbot for EchoAI:
EchoAI is an AI-powered SaaS marketing platform. You've already built the project structure, database schema, authentication, Stripe billing, Facebook campaign agent, and brand discovery agent. Now you need to build the Lead Management system and the AI Lead Qualification Chatbot that engages prospects via voice conversation, qualifies them, and automatically categorizes them as tire kicker, warm, or hot.
In the controllers folder, create a lead controller with the following functions. First, a create lead function that accepts lead name, email, phone, and brand ID, creates a new lead record in the leads table with a default temperature of tire kicker, and triggers the qualification chatbot to start a conversation. Second, a get leads function that returns all leads for a brand filtered by temperature category if specified. Third, an update lead function that allows manually updating lead temperature, conversion status, and contact details. Fourth, a get lead profile function that returns the complete lead record including full conversation history and all CRM interactions. Fifth, a convert lead function that marks a lead as converted and logs the conversion in the CRM interactions table.
In the prompts folder, create a lead qualification prompt file that defines the AI chatbot agent. The chatbot opens with a warm professional greeting on behalf of the business, explains it is here to help and answer questions, and begins a natural voice conversation. It never asks more than one question at a time. It listens carefully to responses and picks up on buying signals such as urgency, budget mentions, specific questions about pricing or timelines, and level of engagement. Based on the conversation it automatically scores the lead internally. If the prospect is vague, disengaged, or just browsing, score them as tire kicker. If they show interest but no urgency, score them as warm. If they ask about pricing, next steps, or express urgency, score them as hot. At the end of the conversation the chatbot thanks them, collects their contact details if not already provided, and saves the full conversation and temperature score to the database. The chatbot tone matches the brand profile of the business it represents once the prospect becomes a customer. Until then it is professional and friendly.
In the controllers folder, add a chatbot controller that manages the conversation flow, calls the Anthropic API using the lead qualification prompt, stores conversation state between exchanges, automatically categorizes the lead based on conversation analysis, and logs every interaction to the CRM interactions table.
In the routes folder, create a leads routes file with POST route for creating a lead, GET route for all leads with optional temperature filter, GET route for a single lead profile, PUT route for updating a lead, POST route for marking a lead as converted, and POST route for the chatbot conversation. Public chatbot route should not require authentication since prospects are not logged in users. All other routes protected with auth and lockout middleware.
Update server dot js to include lead routes at slash api slash leads. Output all files created.
