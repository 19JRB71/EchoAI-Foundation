Here's Prompt 46: AI Email Marketing Campaigns for EchoAI:
EchoAI is an AI-powered SaaS marketing platform. You've built the complete system including the AI Image Generation Studio. Now you need to add AI Email Marketing Campaigns that automatically create, schedule, and send professional email campaigns to leads and customers using the brand's voice, with performance tracking and automated drip sequences.
In the prompts folder create an email marketing prompt file that defines two AI agents. First the Email Campaign Writer Agent that accepts a brand profile, campaign goal such as nurture, promotional, announcement, or re-engagement, target audience segment, and a content topic or offer. It generates a complete email including a compelling subject line with three variations to split test, a preview text under ninety characters, a full email body written in the brand's voice with a clear structure of hook, value, and call to action, and a plain text fallback version. Second the Email Drip Sequence Designer Agent that accepts a brand profile, a campaign goal, and the number of emails requested between three and seven. It designs a complete drip sequence where each email builds on the previous one, escalating engagement and moving the reader toward the desired outcome. Each email in the sequence includes its send delay in days, subject line, preview text, and full body copy.
In the controllers folder create an email marketing controller with the following functions. First a generate campaign email function that accepts brand ID, campaign goal, target segment, and content topic, calls the Anthropic API with the campaign writer agent, and returns a complete email package. Second a generate drip sequence function that accepts brand ID, campaign goal, and number of emails, calls the Anthropic API with the drip sequence designer, and returns a complete multi-email sequence. Third a create campaign function that saves a campaign with its emails and recipient list to the database. Fourth a send campaign function that executes the campaign by sending emails to all recipients using the existing Nodemailer email infrastructure, tracking opens and clicks where possible. Fifth a get campaigns function that returns all email campaigns for a brand with their status, recipient count, open rate, and click rate. Sixth a get campaign detail function that returns a specific campaign with all its emails and per-recipient delivery status. Seventh a create drip sequence function that saves a drip sequence and activates it so emails send automatically on schedule. Eighth an unsubscribe handler that processes unsubscribe requests and marks leads as opted out of email marketing.
In the models folder create a migration file that adds an email campaigns table with columns for campaign ID, brand ID, campaign name, campaign type as one-time or drip, goal, status as draft, scheduled, sending, sent, or paused, recipient count, sent count, open count, click count, scheduled at, sent at, and timestamps. Also add an email campaign emails table with columns for email ID, campaign ID, sequence position, subject line, preview text, body HTML, body plain text, send delay days, and timestamps. Also add an email campaign recipients table with columns for recipient ID, campaign ID, lead ID, email address, delivery status, opened at, clicked at, unsubscribed at, and timestamps.
Wire the unsubscribe handler as a public endpoint at slash api slash email-marketing slash unsubscribe that includes an unsubscribe link at the bottom of every campaign email. Wire opt-out checking so unsubscribed leads never receive email marketing campaigns. Wire the drip sequence scheduler into the existing scheduler system running every hour to check for drip emails that are due and send them.
In the client source folder add a new Email Marketing section to the existing dashboard sidebar under the Marketing group. This section has four tabs. Tab one is Campaigns showing all email campaigns in a table with their name, type, status, recipient count, open rate, and sent date. Show a Create Campaign button that opens a campaign builder. The campaign builder lets users select their brand, choose campaign type as one-time blast or drip sequence, select a goal, choose a recipient segment, enter a content topic, click Generate Email to see the AI-written email with subject line variations, preview the email in a clean rendered format, edit if needed, and send immediately or schedule. For drip sequences show each email in the sequence as a timeline with send delays. Tab two is Drip Sequences showing all active drip sequences with their name, active subscriber count, current step statistics, and pause and cancel controls. Tab three is Contacts showing all leads with their email subscription status and a searchable list. Tab four is Analytics showing total emails sent this month, average open rate, average click rate, and a chart of email activity over the last thirty days.
Apply professional tier gate to email marketing routes since this is a Professional and above feature.
Rebuild the React client after making changes. Output all files created and updated.
