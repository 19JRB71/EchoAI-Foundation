Here's Prompt 55 and 56 Combined: EchoAI Marketing Department Transformation:
EchoAI is an AI-powered SaaS marketing platform. You've built a complete 54-prompt system. Now you need to completely transform the user experience from a feature-based dashboard into an AI Marketing Department where users meet a team of specialized AI employees, each with a name, role, personality, and specific job to do. This transformation makes EchoAI feel like hiring a full marketing department for the cost of a software subscription.
PART 1 — THE AI TEAM
Replace the current dashboard home screen entirely. When a user logs in they no longer see metrics and a sidebar full of features. They meet their AI team. Display eight AI team members as cards on the main screen.
Echo is the Marketing Director and CEO. Echo oversees the entire marketing operation, knows every campaign, every customer, every result, and every opportunity. Echo wakes up every morning and thinks about the business. Echo is the primary voice the user interacts with through the persistent companion panel.
Scout is the Research Specialist. Scout watches competitors, finds trends, identifies new keywords and audiences, monitors what is working in the market, and reports opportunities to the team every week.
Atlas is the Advertising Manager. Atlas builds and manages Facebook ads and Google ads, optimizes budgets, runs split tests, tracks ROI, and never lets a dollar go to waste.
Nova is the Social Media Manager. Nova posts every day, builds content calendars, creates platform-specific content, schedules posts, and keeps the brand visible and consistent across all platforms.
Pulse is the CRM Manager. Pulse never forgets a lead, follows up automatically, books appointments, scores lead temperature, manages the follow-up sequences, and makes sure no prospect falls through the cracks.
Voice is the AI Receptionist. Voice answers the phone, books appointments, qualifies leads, transfers hot prospects, and handles inbound calls around the clock.
Forge is the Creative Director. Forge creates ad images, video scripts, ad copy, social media visuals, and all creative assets using AI generation tools.
Sentinel is the Oversight Agent. Sentinel does not do marketing. Sentinel watches everyone else. Every night Sentinel asks whether Atlas wasted money, whether Nova missed posts, whether Pulse failed to follow up, whether Voice missed calls, whether Forge created low-performing ads. Sentinel fixes problems automatically and reports what it fixed. Sentinel is the quality control layer that makes the whole system reliable.
Each team member card shows their name, title, a simple avatar or icon with a distinct color, their current status as active, working, or needs attention, what they are currently working on, and their results from this week. Clicking a team member card opens their detailed view showing their full activity log, current tasks, completed tasks, and performance metrics.
PART 2 — MISSION CONTROL
Build a Mission Control screen as the command center. This is one screen showing the entire operation at a glance. It should feel like walking into NASA. Show every AI team member with a green, yellow, or red status indicator. Show current tasks running across the organization. Show completed tasks from today. Show revenue and leads generated this week. Show problems identified and fixed by Sentinel. Show upcoming planned actions. Show a morning briefing from Echo at the top of the screen delivered every day when the user logs in that says something like Good morning James. Here is what happened overnight and what I am planning for today. Show an approval queue on the right side showing everything waiting for the user to click approve or decline.
PART 3 — FACEBOOK ONBOARDING WIZARD
Build a step-by-step Facebook setup wizard that guides users through connecting Facebook the first time with zero confusion. This wizard uses screenshots, arrows, and plain English instructions to walk users through every step on Facebook's website. It knows exactly which buttons to tell them to click and in what order. The wizard covers creating a Facebook Business Manager if they do not have one, creating an Ad Account for their business, connecting their Facebook page to the ad account, adding a payment method, and then connecting it all to EchoAI with one click. Once connected EchoAI never needs them to touch Facebook settings again. Everything from that point forward is handled by Atlas automatically.
The wizard should feel like having a patient knowledgeable friend sitting next to you walking you through it. Never use technical jargon. Always show exactly what to click with clear descriptions. Include a progress bar showing which step they are on. If they get stuck show a Help button that opens a live text chat with Echo who can answer any question about the setup process.
PART 4 — PERSISTENT ECHO COMPANION
Build a persistent Echo companion panel that is always visible in the bottom right corner of every screen. This is not a help button. This is Echo — the AI Marketing Director who runs the business. The companion shows a chat interface where Echo proactively updates the user on what is happening, what needs their approval, and what opportunities it has found. Echo speaks first. The user never has to ask what to do next because Echo is already telling them.
When something needs approval Echo shows a preview card inside the companion panel with an Approve and Decline button. The user clicks Approve and Echo executes. That is the only interaction required.
Echo also has a voice button so the user can speak to Echo instead of typing. The user says what they want and Echo does it.
PART 5 — MEMORY
Build a persistent memory system for Echo that remembers everything forever. Every call, every email, every ad, every customer, every complaint, every sale, every meeting, every campaign result. When the user asks Echo what happened with a specific lead or customer Echo can recall the full history. When the user says Echo what happened with Bob, Echo replies with Bob called in March, you quoted him this price, he said he needed financing, he visited the website six more times, last week he viewed the financing page twice, I think he is ready to buy. Store all memory in a searchable database linked to each brand.
PART 6 — AUTONOMOUS GROWTH MODE
Build an Autonomous Growth Mode that the user can enable. The user sets guardrails including monthly ad budget limits, required approval thresholds for spending above a certain amount, brand voice rules, and geographic targeting preferences. Once enabled Echo continuously finds opportunities, proposes changes, executes low-risk optimizations automatically, and requests approval only for high-impact decisions. Echo explains every change it made and why. The user wakes up every morning to a briefing of what Echo did overnight and what it is planning for the day.
PART 7 — UPDATED PRICING
Update the landing page and onboarding to reflect new pricing. Starter is $197 per month. Professional is $497 per month. Enterprise is $997 per month. Additional seats are $50 per month on all tiers. Update all references to old pricing throughout the entire platform including the landing page, onboarding subscription step, feature gate messages, and any other location where pricing appears.
Wire all team members to their corresponding existing backend systems. Echo wires to the setup agent and customer intelligence engine. Scout wires to the competitor intelligence system. Atlas wires to the Facebook campaign controller and Google ads. Nova wires to the social media controller and content calendar. Pulse wires to the CRM, lead controller, follow-up sequences, and appointment controller. Voice wires to the phone agent and voice chatbot. Forge wires to the image studio, ad creative studio, and video content controller. Sentinel wires to the health monitor, scheduler, and all error logging.
Rebuild the React client after all changes. Output all files created and updated.
