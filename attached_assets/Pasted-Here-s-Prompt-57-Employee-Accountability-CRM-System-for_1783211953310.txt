Here's Prompt 57: Employee Accountability CRM System for EchoAI:
EchoAI is an AI-powered SaaS marketing platform. You've built a complete system including the AI Marketing Department with eight named team members. Now you need to build an Employee Accountability CRM System that protects business owners from dishonest employees by controlling exactly what each staff member sees and can do, recording every interaction, and making it impossible for employees to steal leads or contact information.
PART 1 — ROLE BASED ACCESS WHEN INVITING STAFF
Update the team member invitation system to include three clearly defined roles. When inviting a team member the owner selects one of three roles from a dropdown.
Admin sees everything the owner sees except they cannot delete the account, cannot change billing, and cannot remove the owner. Admin has full read and write access to all departments except Sentinel.
Manager sees everything across all departments but has read only access. A manager cannot create, edit, delete, or change anything. They can view every campaign, every lead, every call recording, every report, and every piece of data but they cannot touch it. This is perfect for a supervisor who needs visibility without the ability to interfere.
Sales Rep sees only the CRM system and only one lead at a time. They cannot browse the lead list. They cannot see other leads. They work through leads one at a time in a queue system. When they complete a task on a lead the next lead is handed to them automatically.
Update the invitation email to clearly state which role the person is being invited as and what they have access to.
PART 2 — SALES REP ONE LEAD AT A TIME QUEUE
Build a completely separate CRM experience for Sales Rep role users. When a Sales Rep logs in they do not see the AI Marketing Department or Mission Control. They see a single focused screen showing one lead at a time.
The screen shows at the top a queue counter such as You have 14 leads waiting but does not show any names, phone numbers, or details of the waiting leads. Below the counter is the current lead they are working on.
The current lead shows the lead's first name only initially. It shows a masked phone number where the middle digits are replaced with X such as 352-XXX-1008. It shows the lead temperature badge. It shows any notes from previous interactions. It shows the conversation history from the chatbot if available.
Below the lead information show a large Call button. When the Sales Rep clicks Call the system automatically dials the lead's full phone number through Twilio without ever showing the full number to the Sales Rep. The call is routed through EchoAI, recorded automatically, and the full transcript is saved to the lead profile.
After the call ends or if the Sales Rep is logging a different type of interaction show a task completion panel with the following outcome options. Called and gave quote. Called and left message. Called and customer not interested. Booked appointment. Customer requested callback. Transferred to manager. Each outcome option has a notes field where the Sales Rep can add details. When they select an outcome and click Complete and Next the current lead is marked with that outcome, the timestamp and Sales Rep ID are logged, and the next lead in the queue is shown automatically.
If the Sales Rep tries to navigate away from this screen without completing a task show a warning that they must complete the current lead before moving on.
PART 3 — PHONE NUMBER MASKING
Implement phone number masking across the entire platform for Sales Rep role users. When a Sales Rep views any screen that shows a phone number the number is masked. The first three digits of the area code are visible, the middle six digits are replaced with X, and the last two digits are visible. The full phone number is stored in the database and used by the system for dialing but is never displayed to Sales Rep users.
When a Sales Rep clicks the Call button the system retrieves the full number from the database, dials it through Twilio, connects the Sales Rep to the call, records the entire conversation, and saves the recording and transcript to the lead profile. The Sales Rep hears the call through their browser or phone but never sees the full number on screen.
PART 4 — CALL RECORDING AND ACCOUNTABILITY LOG
Every call made through EchoAI by any team member is automatically recorded using Twilio's recording feature. The recording is saved to the lead profile along with the transcript, the duration, the timestamp, and the name of the team member who made the call.
Build an accountability log for each lead that shows every interaction in chronological order including who made the call, when, how long it lasted, what outcome was logged, what notes were added, and a link to the call recording. This log is visible to the owner and admin only. Sales Reps and Managers cannot see the full accountability log.
Build an admin call monitoring panel inside the Sentinel department that shows all calls made by all team members today with their recording links. The owner can listen to any call from any employee at any time. Show a summary of each team member's activity including how many leads they worked today, how many calls they made, how many appointments they booked, and how many quotes they gave.
PART 5 — LEAD QUEUE MANAGEMENT
Build a lead queue management system for the owner and admin. The owner can see all leads in the queue and assign them to specific Sales Reps or leave them unassigned for automatic distribution. When a Sales Rep completes a lead the system automatically assigns the next unassigned lead in the queue to them based on the order they came in. The owner can reprioritize leads in the queue, move leads between Sales Reps, and remove leads from the queue.
Build a queue overview in the Pulse department for owners and admins showing how many leads each Sales Rep has in their queue, how many they completed today, and their current lead status. This gives the owner a real time view of their sales team's activity without having to ask anyone what they are doing.
PART 6 — MANAGER READ ONLY ENFORCEMENT
Enforce read only access for Manager role across every part of the platform. Every form, button, input field, and action control is disabled for Manager users. They can see everything but cannot interact with anything that would create, modify, or delete data. Show a subtle read only badge in the top navigation bar when a Manager is logged in so they always know their role. If a Manager tries to perform a write action show a friendly message explaining they have read only access and to contact the account owner if they need to make changes.
PART 7 — ROLE DISPLAY AND TRANSPARENCY
Show each team member's role clearly in the top navigation bar when they are logged in. Sales Rep sees a badge saying Sales Rep. Manager sees a badge saying Manager. Admin sees a badge saying Admin. Owner sees a badge saying Owner. This removes any confusion about what access level they have.
Update the team management panel in settings to show each team member's role, their last login date, how many leads they worked this week, and how many calls they made this week. Give the owner a Deactivate button next to each team member that immediately revokes their access without deleting their history.
Wire all call recording into the existing Twilio infrastructure. Wire the lead queue into the existing leads database. Wire the accountability log into the existing CRM interactions table. Wire phone masking into every component that displays phone numbers for Sales Rep users.
Rebuild the React client after all changes. Output all files created and updated.
