Zorecho Landing Page Hero — Interactive Echo Demo

We want the landing page hero to use the same Zorecho Core visual language as the new Mission Control experience.

The landing page should not merely explain the product. It should demonstrate what it feels like to have an AI company working for you.

Hero structure
Left side

Headline:

Meet Your AI Company.

Supporting copy:

Zorecho brings your marketing, sales, customer calls, follow-up, research, and business intelligence together in one connected AI workforce.

Primary CTA:

Start Your AI Company

Secondary CTA:

Watch Echo Work

Right side

Display the animated Zorecho Core.

The Zorecho Core represents the central intelligence.

Echo remains the user’s personal AI assistant and communicates through the Core.

Interactive demo behavior

On initial page load:

The Core appears in a calm idle state.
Do not autoplay audible speech.
Show a prominent button labeled Meet Echo or Watch Echo Work.

When the visitor starts the demo:

The Core enters speaking mode.

Echo says:

“Hello. I’m Echo, your personal AI assistant.”

Echo continues:

“Good morning, sir. Here’s your example business briefing.”

The demo presents an example briefing:
Ten new leads arrived overnight.
Seven customer emails need attention.
Voice answered twelve calls.
Three appointments were booked.
Scout found two new competitor campaigns.
Nova prepared today’s social content.
As each department is mentioned:
its permanent agent color illuminates
its connection to the Core pulses
a small report card appears beside the Core

Echo closes with:

“Your AI company is online and ready. Would you like to see Mission Control?”

Display CTA:

See Mission Control

Honesty requirement

This is a product demonstration, not real visitor data.

Clearly label it:

Example Zorecho Morning Briefing

Do not imply the sample values are live.

Core states

Use the same shared Core component as Mission Control:

Idle
Listening
Thinking
Speaking
Agent activity
Attention

Do not create a visually different landing-page Core.

The landing page and backend must feel like one continuous product.

Controls

Include:

Play
Pause
Replay
Mute/unmute
Skip demo
Motion

Keep the experience premium, calm, and purposeful.

No excessive flashing.

No loud autoplay.

No animation that delays the visitor from using the page.

Immediate request

Before implementation, provide:

Hero component breakdown
Desktop layout plan
Mobile adaptation
Audio/demo sequence plan
Confirmation that the Mission Control Core component will be reused

Do not modify the rest of the landing page yet.

Build only the hero behind a preview or development route and return desktop and mobile screenshots for approval.

This gives the landing page a strong first impression while making the transition into Mission Control feel natural: visitors meet Echo outside, then enter the headquarters inside.
