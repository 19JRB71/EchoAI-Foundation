Here's Prompt 22: Billing Polish and Subscription Management for EchoAI:
EchoAI is an AI-powered SaaS marketing platform. You've built the complete system. Now you need to polish the billing and subscription management experience so customers can easily upgrade, downgrade, view their billing history, and manage their payment methods without needing to contact you.
In the controllers folder update the existing subscription controller with the following additional functions. First an upgrade or downgrade subscription function that accepts a new subscription tier, updates the subscription in Stripe, updates the database, and immediately reflects the change in the customer's account access. Second a update payment method function that accepts a new payment method ID from Stripe Elements, attaches it to the customer in Stripe, sets it as the default, and updates the database. Third a get billing history function that pulls the last twelve invoices from Stripe for the customer and returns them with date, amount, status, and a download link for the PDF invoice. Fourth a get upcoming invoice function that returns what the customer will be charged on their next billing date based on their current plan and seat count.
In the client source folder update the existing Settings section to add a dedicated Billing tab. This tab has four parts. First a current plan card showing their active tier, price per month, next billing date, and number of seats used. Include an upgrade and downgrade button that opens a plan selector modal showing all four tiers with their features and prices. Second a payment method card showing the last four digits of their card on file, expiry date, and an Update Payment Method button that opens a Stripe Elements card input form. Third a billing history table showing the last twelve invoices with date, amount, status as paid or failed, and a Download Invoice link for each one. Fourth an upcoming invoice card showing exactly what they will be charged on their next billing date.
Also add a prominent banner at the top of the dashboard that appears only when a payment has failed. The banner should be red, say their payment failed and their account will be locked in X days, and include a button to update their payment method immediately. This banner should appear on every page of the dashboard until the payment issue is resolved.
Wire all new endpoints to the backend. Rebuild the React client. Output all files created and updated.
