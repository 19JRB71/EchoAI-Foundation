Response to the Audit
Decision 1 — Pricing

Keep the current pricing:

Starter: $197/month
Professional: $497/month
Enterprise: $997/month

Do not lower pricing during private testing.

We can always adjust pricing later after gathering customer feedback and understanding actual operating costs.

Decision 2 — Included Capacity

I strongly recommend using internal dollar-cost accounting, not tokens or arbitrary units.

Customers should only ever see:

AI Workforce Capacity

For example:

AI Workforce Capacity

██████████░░░░░

62% Used

Internally, we track dollars.

Customers never see tokens or internal costs.

This gives us the flexibility to change providers, models, or pricing without redesigning the customer experience.

Decision 3 — Overage Behavior

During private testing:

Notify only.

No automatic billing.

No feature lockouts.

No forced upgrades.

The purpose of private testing is to learn how customers actually use the system.

Once we understand real-world usage patterns, we can finalize overage policies before the public launch.

Decision 4 — Background Scan Frequency

Absolutely.

Background intelligence should scale with the customer's plan.

Example:

Starter
Scout research once daily
Sage industry intelligence once daily
Minimal background monitoring
Professional
Research several times throughout the day
More proactive monitoring
More frequent optimization
Enterprise
Near real-time monitoring where it provides meaningful value
Highest level of automation and intelligence

This protects margins while giving higher-tier customers more value.

Decision 5 — Voice Tiers

Do not make Voice a separate product.

Voice should be one of the reasons people upgrade plans.

Suggested mapping:

Starter

Echo Standard

Short conversations
Business questions
Platform guidance
Appointment handling
Lower-cost reasoning
Professional

Echo Professional

Longer conversations
Better reasoning
Sales conversations
Marketing discussions
Better follow-up
Enterprise

Echo Executive

Executive-level reasoning
Strategic planning
Long-form consulting
Deep business analysis
Full "Jarvis" experience

This makes each subscription level feel more valuable instead of creating another add-on to explain.

Decision 6 — Provider Reconciliation

For now:

Use estimated internal costs.

When provider billing APIs or organization-level reconciliation credentials become available, add automated reconciliation as a later phase.

Private testing does not require perfect financial reconciliation.

The architecture should support it, but we should not delay beta because of it.
