Here's Prompt 68: Sage — The Industry Intelligence Agent for EchoAI:
EchoAI is an AI-powered SaaS marketing platform. You've built a complete AI Marketing Department with eight named team members. Now you need to add the ninth and final member — Sage, the Industry Intelligence Agent. Sage has one job and one job only: study every industry your customers operate in at a deep level, never stop learning, and feed that intelligence to every other agent so the entire team gets smarter every single day.
PART 1 — SAGE'S IDENTITY AND ROLE
Add Sage as the ninth AI team member. Sage's title is Industry Intelligence Agent. Sage's avatar color is deep emerald green. Sage's description is "Studying your industry around the clock so your team always has the smartest possible strategy."
Add Sage to the AI team sidebar between Sentinel and the bottom of the navigation. Add Sage's card to Mission Control team status. Add Sage's department view with all relevant tools. Sage's status is always Active because Sage never stops working.
Sage is fundamentally different from every other team member. Every other agent acts on your business. Sage studies the world around your business and reports back what it finds. Sage is your unfair advantage — the intelligence that makes every other agent smarter than they could ever be on their own.
PART 2 — WHAT SAGE STUDIES
When a new brand is created and the Setup Agent completes Sage immediately begins studying that brand's industry. Sage builds a comprehensive industry intelligence profile covering the following areas.
Industry landscape — the major players, market size, growth trends, and where the industry is heading over the next 12 to 24 months.
Consumer psychology — what motivates buyers in this industry, what their fears and desires are, what language resonates with them, what objections they raise most frequently, and what triggers purchasing decisions.
Marketing best practices — what advertising approaches are working right now in this industry, what headlines and hooks are getting the most engagement, what visual styles are resonating, what platforms are most effective, and what offers are converting best.
Seasonal patterns — when demand peaks and troughs throughout the year, what events and dates drive purchasing decisions, and how to position marketing ahead of seasonal opportunities.
Regulatory environment — what advertising rules apply to this industry, what claims cannot be made, what disclosures are required, and what platform policies specifically affect marketing in this space.
Competitive intelligence — what the broader competitive landscape looks like beyond just direct competitors, what strategies are being used industry wide, and where gaps exist that the owner's business can exploit.
Emerging opportunities — new technologies, new platforms, new consumer behaviors, and new market segments that are emerging in this industry that the owner could capitalize on early.
PART 3 — HOW SAGE LEARNS
Sage uses web search to continuously gather intelligence. Every six hours Sage runs a set of targeted searches specific to each brand's industry. Sage searches for industry news, recent research, consumer sentiment, trending topics, competitor announcements, regulatory changes, and marketing case studies relevant to each industry.
Sage reads and analyzes every result, extracts the most relevant intelligence, and adds it to the brand's industry knowledge base. Over time this knowledge base grows into a deep and comprehensive understanding of the industry that no human researcher could match because Sage never stops and never forgets.
Sage tracks what it has already read so it never wastes time on the same information twice. Sage always prioritizes fresh intelligence — articles and research published in the last 30 days get more weight than older material.
When Sage finds something particularly significant — a major competitor announcement, a regulatory change, a viral trend, or a major market shift — it flags it immediately as urgent intelligence and delivers it to Echo right away rather than waiting for the next scheduled briefing.
PART 4 — HOW SAGE FEEDS THE OTHER AGENTS
Sage shares its intelligence with every other agent through a shared knowledge base that all agents can access.
Atlas receives Sage's marketing best practices intelligence. Before building any ad campaign Atlas checks what Sage has learned about what is working in that industry right now. Ad copy, headlines, hooks, visual direction, and audience targeting all get informed by Sage's latest research. Atlas never builds a campaign in a vacuum — it always builds on what Sage knows is working.
Nova receives Sage's trending topic intelligence and content best practices. Before scheduling any social media post Nova checks what Sage has learned about what content is resonating in that industry. Nova uses Sage's seasonal patterns to time content for maximum impact.
Pulse receives Sage's consumer psychology intelligence. Follow-up messages, email sequences, and SMS campaigns all get informed by Sage's understanding of what language and offers resonate with buyers in that industry. Pulse uses Sage's objection intelligence to craft follow-ups that address the most common hesitations before they are even raised.
Forge receives Sage's creative direction intelligence. Every image generated and every video script written gets informed by Sage's knowledge of what visual styles and messaging are resonating in that industry right now.
Scout receives Sage's competitive landscape intelligence to make its competitor monitoring sharper and more contextually aware.
Echo incorporates Sage's intelligence into every morning briefing and every strategic recommendation. When Echo suggests an action it can now say "based on what Sage has learned about the home building industry this approach typically generates 40 percent more leads than the alternative."
PART 5 — SAGE'S DEPARTMENT VIEW
Build Sage's department view with four tabs.
Tab one is Industry Brief showing the current comprehensive industry intelligence profile for the selected brand. Show it organized into sections — Industry Landscape, Consumer Psychology, Marketing Best Practices, Seasonal Patterns, Regulatory Environment, Competitive Intelligence, and Emerging Opportunities. Each section shows Sage's latest findings in plain English with the date last updated. Show a Refresh button that triggers Sage to run a fresh research cycle immediately.
Tab two is Latest Intelligence showing a chronological feed of everything Sage has learned in the last 30 days. Each item shows the date, the source type such as industry news or competitor announcement or consumer trend, a plain English summary of what was learned, and why it matters for the owner's business. Show an Urgent badge on items Sage flagged as immediately significant.
Tab three is Marketing Insights showing Sage's specific recommendations for marketing based on its industry research. What headlines are working. What hooks are converting. What offers are resonating. What platforms are most effective right now. What seasonal opportunities are coming up in the next 30 to 60 days. This is the most actionable tab — pure intelligence the owner can use immediately.
Tab four is Knowledge Base showing a searchable archive of everything Sage has ever learned about this industry. The owner can search for any topic and Sage retrieves all relevant intelligence it has accumulated. This grows more valuable every week as Sage accumulates more knowledge.
PART 6 — SAGE IN THE MORNING BRIEFING
Echo includes a Sage Intelligence Update in every morning briefing. After covering overnight business activity Echo says something like Sage has been studying the home building market overnight and found something worth your attention. Search volume for affordable home kits in Central Florida increased 34 percent this week. Sage recommends Atlas increase ad spend by 20 percent this week to capture this demand spike. Want me to make that adjustment?
When Sage finds urgent intelligence Echo delivers it immediately as a voice alert regardless of the time of day. James Sage just flagged something important. A new competitor just launched in your market with aggressive pricing. Sage has already analyzed their strategy and found three gaps we can exploit. Want to hear them?
PART 7 — SAGE'S CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT
Sage gets smarter about each industry the longer it studies it. Sage tracks which of its intelligence recommendations led to improved campaign performance and which did not. Over time Sage learns what types of intelligence are most valuable for each specific business and prioritizes gathering more of that type.
Sage also learns the owner's preferences. If the owner always acts on seasonal trend intelligence but rarely acts on regulatory updates Sage prioritizes seasonal research and reduces time spent on regulatory monitoring for that owner.
Every month Sage generates a monthly industry intelligence report summarizing the most important things it learned that month, how that intelligence was applied across the team, and what it predicts will be the biggest opportunities and risks in the coming month.
PART 8 — SAGE FOR ECHOAI ITSELF
Since EchoAI is itself a business Sage studies the AI marketing platform industry on behalf of the platform owner. Sage monitors what competitors like GoHighLevel are doing, what new AI marketing tools are emerging, what features are being most requested by small business owners, and what pricing strategies are working in the SaaS marketing space. This intelligence goes directly to the platform owner — James — to inform EchoAI's own product development and marketing strategy.
Wire Sage's research scheduler to run every six hours using the existing scheduler infrastructure. Wire Sage's knowledge base into every other agent's decision making process. Wire Sage's urgent intelligence alerts into the existing voice alert system. Wire Sage's morning briefing contribution into the existing echo briefing system. Add Sage to the Mission Control team status grid. Add Sage to the AI team sidebar. Build Sage's complete department view with all four tabs.
