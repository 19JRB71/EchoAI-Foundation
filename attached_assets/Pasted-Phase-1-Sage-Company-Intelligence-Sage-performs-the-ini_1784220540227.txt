Phase 1: Sage Company Intelligence

Sage performs the initial company discovery process and creates a comprehensive Company Intelligence Report containing:

Company identity and contact information
Website and connected social accounts
Industry and exact business classification
Products and services
Service area
Target customers
Business model
Pricing or offer structure where available
Company values and promises
Strengths and differentiators
Approved competitors
Industry terminology
Excluded or commonly confused business categories
Public reputation and review themes
Uploaded documents, photos, videos, and other authorized assets
Current marketing activity
Opportunities, threats, and missing information

Sage must present the completed report to the customer in plain language.

The customer must choose:

Approve
Edit
Request additional research

The report may not be distributed to other departments until the customer explicitly approves it.

Phase 2: Approved Company Truth

Once approved, the Company Intelligence Report becomes the authoritative Company Truth for that customer.

Echo distributes this approved report to every department head.

No department head or subordinate agent may redefine the company’s industry, products, audience, competitors, terminology, or business model independently.

Any conflict must be escalated to Sage and the customer for correction.

Phase 3: Department-Specific Understanding

Each department head must interpret the approved Company Truth according to its own responsibilities and build a Department Understanding Profile.

Each profile must define:

Department mission
Department goals
Applicable company information
Required tools and connected accounts
Approved workflows
Automatic actions
Actions requiring customer approval
Prohibited actions
Escalation rules
Quality standards
Success metrics
Missing information
Relationship with other departments

The department head must guide the customer to the correct screen, account connection, or upload area when information is missing.

Phase 4: Department Approval

Each department head presents a short Department Understanding Report to the customer.

The report must clearly explain:

What the department understands
What it plans to do
What it may do automatically
What requires approval
What it will never do without permission
What information or connections are still missing

The customer must approve or correct the department profile before autonomous work begins.

Phase 5: Chain-of-Command Execution

All work follows this cycle:

Understand → Plan → Delegate → Execute → Review → Approve → Report

Sub-agents and tools receive assignments from their department head.

Their outputs return to that department head for review.

No subordinate result may move directly to another department or to the customer.

The department head must validate that the work matches:

The approved Company Truth
The department profile
The customer’s goals
Brand and compliance rules
Current task instructions

The department head may reject the work, correct the assignment, and send it back for revision.

Cross-Department Communication

Cross-department work must move department head to department head.

Example:

Sage identifies a proven marketing pattern.

Sage validates it against the approved Company Truth.

Sage sends an intelligence brief to Forge.

Forge interprets it through the approved Creative Department Profile.

Forge delegates production.

Forge reviews the result.

Atlas receives the approved campaign only after Forge approves it.

Sentinel may audit any stage.

Echo coordinates priorities, resolves conflicts, and reports outcomes to the customer.

Version Control

Whenever the business changes, Sage creates a proposed update to the Company Truth.

Changes must not silently overwrite approved information.

The customer reviews and approves the new version.

Approved updates are then distributed to every department head, and each affected department updates its profile accordingly.

Core Rule

The named AI agents are executives of their departments.

They do not merely perform tasks.

They understand, direct, validate, reject, correct, and report.

Sage establishes the approved company-wide truth. Each department head then develops an approved specialized understanding of how its department should serve that company.
