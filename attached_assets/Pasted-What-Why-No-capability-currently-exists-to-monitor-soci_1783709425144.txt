What & Why
No capability currently exists to monitor social platforms for people actively signaling buying intent (e.g., "looking for a house shell kit near Ocala," "need extra storage space," posting in local buy/sell/rec groups). James wants Sage (Industry Intelligence Agent) to scan Facebook/Instagram, Twitter/X, and Reddit for these signals per brand, surface them as alerts, and automatically add qualified signals to the CRM as leads for Pulse to work.
Done looks like

Sage continuously (or on a scheduled interval) scans Facebook/Instagram, Twitter/X, and Reddit for posts/comments indicating active buying intent relevant to each brand's business (e.g., Blacor Homes: people discussing home shells, land + build, downsizing, moving to Florida; South Dixie Storage: people discussing storage needs, decluttering, moving).
Each brand has its own search criteria/keywords — scoped per brand, not shared across businesses (ties into brand-isolation requirement).
When a qualifying signal is found, two things happen automatically:

Alert — business owner is notified (dashboard notification + ties into existing notification badge system from #103) with the post content, platform, and link/context.
CRM entry — the lead is automatically added to Pulse's CRM with source ("Social Listening"), platform, and the original post content/context attached, so it's immediately workable.


Sage can distinguish genuine buying-intent signals from noise (e.g., "I hate storage units" vs. "looking for a storage unit near me") — some baseline relevance/intent filtering, not just raw keyword matching.
Duplicate detection — the same post/person isn't alerted or added to CRM multiple times.
Setup includes a way for the business owner to define/adjust what counts as a buying signal for their brand (keywords, phrases, or a description Sage uses to judge intent).

Out of scope

Automatically messaging or engaging the person who posted (no auto-DM/auto-reply to strangers on social platforms) — this ticket surfaces leads for the human/CRM to act on, it does not initiate contact.
Platforms beyond Facebook/Instagram, Twitter/X, and Reddit for v1.
Paid social listening API integrations requiring separate contracts/approval — use available/free-tier APIs or existing connections where possible; flag if a paid data source is required before building against it.

Steps

Define per-brand buying-signal criteria — build a way for the business owner to input relevant keywords/phrases/description (or provide sensible defaults per business type).
Build Sage's scanning logic for Facebook/Instagram, Twitter/X, and Reddit — using available public APIs (Reddit API, X API, Meta's public content where accessible) within their terms of service.
Build intent-relevance filtering so Sage distinguishes real buying signals from unrelated mentions (not pure keyword matching).
Build duplicate detection so the same signal isn't alerted/added twice.
Wire matched signals into the existing notification system (ties to #103 badges) to alert the business owner.
Wire matched signals into Pulse's CRM as a new lead, tagged with source, platform, and original post context.
Verify strict brand isolation — one brand's social listening criteria/results never surface under another brand.
Flag clearly if any data source requires a paid tier/contract before implementation, rather than building against an assumption.
