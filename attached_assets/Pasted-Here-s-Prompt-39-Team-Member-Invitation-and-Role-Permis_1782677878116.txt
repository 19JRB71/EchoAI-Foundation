Here's Prompt 39: Team Member Invitation and Role Permissions for EchoAI:
EchoAI is an AI-powered SaaS marketing platform. You've built the complete system with 38 prompts worth of features. Now you need to build a proper Team Member Invitation and Role Permissions system so business owners can invite their staff, assign roles, and control what each team member can see and do inside the platform.
There are three team member roles. Viewer can see leads, reports, analytics, and the CRM but cannot launch campaigns, post content, make calls, or change any settings. Manager can do everything a Viewer can plus launch campaigns, generate content, manage leads, use the voice chatbot, phone agent, and all AI tools, but cannot access billing, subscription management, or account settings. Admin can do everything a Manager can plus manage team members, access billing and subscription settings, and change account configuration. The account owner has full unrestricted access to everything. The platform admin identified by the admin role in the users table bypasses all role checks entirely.
In the models folder create a migration file that adds a team members table with columns for team member ID, account owner user ID, invited user ID, email, role as viewer, manager, or admin, status as pending, active, or removed, invited at, accepted at, and timestamps. Also add a team invitations table with columns for invitation ID, account owner user ID, invited email, role, secure token as a unique string, expires at, and timestamps.
In the controllers folder create a team controller with the following functions. First an invite member function that accepts an email address and role, checks that the account owner has available seats based on their subscription tier, creates a secure invitation token, stores the invitation record, sends a professional branded invitation email with a secure one-time link that expires in forty eight hours, and if the invited email already has an EchoAI account links them immediately. Second an accept invitation function that is called when the invited person clicks their invitation link, validates the token has not expired, creates their account if they do not have one, links them to the account owner's workspace, sets their role, and redirects them to the dashboard. Third a get team members function that returns all active and pending team members for an account with their role, status, and last active date. Fourth an update member role function that allows the account owner or admin to change a team member's role. Fifth a remove member function that removes a team member from the workspace, revokes their access immediately, and updates the seat count triggering a Stripe subscription adjustment if applicable. Sixth a resend invitation function that generates a new invitation token and resends the invitation email if the original expired.
Create a role permissions middleware file in the middleware folder that checks the authenticated user's role within the workspace before allowing access to role-restricted actions. Viewer role blocks access to any POST, PUT, PATCH, or DELETE routes that modify campaigns, content, leads status changes, calls, or social posts. Manager role blocks access to billing routes, subscription routes, and team management routes. Admin role blocks access to account deletion only. Account owners and platform admins bypass all role checks.
In the client source folder add a Team Management section to the Settings panel. This section shows a list of all team members with their name, email, role displayed as a colored badge, status as active or pending, and last active date. Show an Invite Team Member button that opens a modal asking for email address and role selection with a clear description of what each role can do. Show a Resend Invite button next to pending members. Show a Remove Member button and a Change Role dropdown next to active members. Show the current seat count and what it means for billing — for example showing three of five included seats used with a note that adding more will cost fifty dollars per additional seat per month.
Update the dashboard so team members who are not the account owner see the platform branded with the account owner's business name and brand profile rather than their own. Team members should feel like they are working inside their employer's platform.
Update the onboarding flow to include an optional team setup step after subscription selection where the account owner can invite their first team members during initial setup.
Wire the seat count into the Stripe subscription so adding a team member beyond the included seats automatically triggers a fifty dollar per seat monthly charge adjustment and removing a team member triggers a credit adjustment at the next billing cycle.
The platform admin account is completely exempt from all role checks, seat limits, billing adjustments, and invitation requirements.
Output all files created and updated.
