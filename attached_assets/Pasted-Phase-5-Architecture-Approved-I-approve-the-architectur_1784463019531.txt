Phase 5 Architecture Approved.

I approve the architecture as presented.

More importantly, I think the design philosophy is correct.

Why I'm approving it
1. Evidence is structural—not prompt-based. ✅

This is probably the strongest part of the architecture.

"No evidence, no opportunity."

Making that a database rule instead of an AI instruction is exactly the right design.

The AI shouldn't be able to invent opportunities simply because it "feels confident."

The fact that:

opportunities reference actual intelligence,
confidence is derived from evidence,
evidence is foreign-keyed,

means the system becomes far more trustworthy.

2. Explainability is built into the model. ✅

I especially like this:

"Why is Sage recommending this?"

Every recommendation should answer that question immediately.

That will help users trust Sage.

It will also help you debug Sage years from now.

3. Advisory first. ✅

This matches your vision from the beginning.

Sage isn't replacing the owner.

Sage becomes the executive advisor.

The owner still decides.

Departments still execute.

That's a healthy chain of command.

4. The audit chain is excellent. ✅

This is probably the most valuable long-term feature.

Evidence
      ↓
Opportunity
      ↓
Decision
      ↓
Directive
      ↓
Result
      ↓
Lesson

That becomes the institutional memory of the business.

Very few AI platforms are thinking at that level.

5. Change Diagnostics

I love that it's arithmetic first.

That prevents AI from inventing reasons.

Instead it becomes:

SQL computes facts.

AI explains facts.

Exactly right.

One addition I'd like

I would like every opportunity to include a confidence explanation, not just a confidence score.

Example:

Confidence: 82%

Reason:

Competitor increased Facebook spend (verified)
Lead conversion declined 11%
Roofing demand increased in service area
Based on 41 comparable opportunities

That explanation doesn't have to be AI generated.

Most of it can be assembled deterministically from the evidence graph.

That makes confidence understandable instead of just a number.

I consider this a refinement within Phase 5—not a scope change.

Another suggestion

I'd also recommend every opportunity carry one additional field internally:

Opportunity Status

Example lifecycle:

New
Reviewed
Approved
Rejected
Assigned
In Progress
Completed
Archived

That creates a clean executive workflow.

If that's already in the architecture, excellent. If not, it's still consistent with Phase 5 because it governs the queue itself rather than adding new intelligence capabilities.
