Onboarding Audit Approved — Refine, Don't Rebuild

Excellent work on the audit.

After reviewing the current onboarding architecture, I do not want to rebuild the onboarding experience.

The existing architecture is approved.

Our objective is now to transform it from a setup wizard into an experience where customers genuinely feel that Echo is personally helping them build their AI company.

Priority 1 — Echo Should Feel Alive

Keep the current onboarding flow exactly as it is.

Instead, improve Echo's interaction throughout the process.

Echo should:

Speak naturally instead of sounding scripted.
Explain why each step matters.
Congratulate the customer when a connection succeeds.
Reassure the customer if a connection fails.
Smoothly transition to the next step.

Example:

"Excellent. Your Facebook account is connected."

"Nova can now begin preparing your social media."

"Let's move on to Google."

The customer should feel like Echo is working beside them.

Priority 2 — Connection Confirmations

Whenever an integration succeeds, Echo should immediately acknowledge it.

Examples:

Facebook connected

Google connected

Calendar connected

Phone connected

Website analyzed

CRM connected

The customer should never wonder whether something worked.

Priority 3 — Finish the Thin Sections

The audit identified that Team and Completion have minimal narration.

Expand these.

Completion should become a celebration.

Example:

"Congratulations."

"Your AI company is now online."

"While you enjoy your coffee, my team is already getting to work."

"Would you like a quick tour of Mission Control?"

Priority 4 — Automatic Mission Control Tour

When onboarding completes:

Automatically ask:

"Would you like me to give you a two-minute tour?"

If yes:

Launch the guided Mission Control walkthrough.

If no:

Allow the tour to be started later.

Priority 5 — Setup Checklist

The audit mentioned additional integrations that are not represented yet.

Instead of redesigning onboarding, create a visible Setup Checklist.

Examples:

Facebook

Instagram

Google

Calendar

Phone

Website

CRM

Business Profile

Email

Google Business Profile

Completed items receive a green check.

Missing items remain available later.

Priority 6 — Skip Behavior

Keep "Do Later" during private beta.

However:

When a customer skips onboarding,

Mission Control should clearly indicate:

"Your AI company is operating with limited capabilities until setup is completed."

Echo should occasionally remind the customer about missing connections without becoming annoying.

Priority 7 — Personalization

Before onboarding finishes,

Echo should ask several personalization questions such as:

How involved would you like me to be?
Do you prefer daily briefings?
Should I notify you immediately about important issues?
Do you prefer concise or detailed explanations?

These preferences should become part of Echo's personality for that customer.

Priority 8 — Do Not Rebuild

The onboarding architecture is approved.

Do not redesign the wizard.

Do not rewrite working components.

Focus only on making the experience feel more conversational, more personal, and more polished.

Deliverables

Before implementing, provide:

The specific onboarding screens that will change.
The new Echo dialogue for each screen.
Screenshots of any visual changes.
The updated onboarding flow.

After approval, implement the changes.
