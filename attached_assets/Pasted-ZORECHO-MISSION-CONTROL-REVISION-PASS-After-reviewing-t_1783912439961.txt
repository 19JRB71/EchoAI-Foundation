ZORECHO MISSION CONTROL – REVISION PASS

After reviewing the latest build, we want to make one architectural improvement before continuing.

The overall direction is much better, but we want Mission Control to feel like a true AI headquarters rather than repeating the same information twice.

IMPORTANT

Do not redesign the page.

Do not change the visual style.

Do not move the overall layout.

Continue using the current Mission Control concept as the visual target.

This revision is about improving the information architecture, not changing the design.

CHANGE 1 – Remove the duplicate agent list

The center of Mission Control is now the official visualization of the AI workforce.

Because of that, the left sidebar should no longer display all nine agents.

The agent network surrounding the Zorecho Core already communicates that information.

Displaying the same roster twice wastes premium screen space.

CHANGE 2 – Replace the sidebar with an Executive Command Panel

Instead of nine duplicate agent cards, redesign the left sidebar into an executive control area.

Suggested structure:

Echo Assistant (top section)

Echo remains visible because Echo is the user's primary assistant.

Display:

Echo avatar/icon
Current status
Current activity
Quick microphone indicator

Echo should always feel available.

AI Workforce Summary

Replace the agent list with a live company summary.

Examples:

AI Workforce Online
Agents Active
Tasks Completed Today
Campaigns Running
Conversations Active
System Health

Use real platform data whenever available.

Businesses

Display the user's businesses.

Examples:

South Dixie Storage
Blacor Homes
Additional businesses

This becomes the primary business switcher.

Quick Actions

Include frequently used actions.

Examples:

Talk to Echo
Create Campaign
New Task
Reports
Settings
Brand Panel

Keep the premium Zorecho branding panel at the bottom.

CHANGE 3 – Make the Core the Hero

The center visualization is now the identity of the product.

Increase emphasis on:

Zorecho Core
Agent network
Connection lines
Talk to Echo

This should be the first thing every customer notices.

The Core represents the entire AI company.

Echo is the assistant that communicates with the owner.

Those are two separate concepts.

DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

Mission Control should immediately communicate:

"My AI company is operating while I focus on my business."

Everything on screen should support that feeling.

Remove duplicated information whenever possible.

Prioritize clarity, hierarchy, and premium presentation over displaying more information.

IMPORTANT

Do not continue building additional sections until this revision is complete.

Return one updated desktop screenshot for approval before proceeding to the next implementation pass.

I think this is a stronger direction because it gives every part of the screen a unique purpose:

Center: Your AI company (the nine-agent workforce).
Left: Your executive command center and interaction with Echo.
Right: Intelligence, alerts, and executive insights.
Bottom: Performance and business analytics.

Nothing is duplicated, and every section earns its space. That's the kind of interface that feels intentional and easier to understand the first time someone sees it.
